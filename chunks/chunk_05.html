Chunk 5

Pages 16-18

The chunk continues the **Spanish** chapter amid the Dispute of Tortosa, where anti-Jewish and anti-Christian apologetics answered each other. Secret cites Scipion du Pleix on metempsychosis, then **Jerónimo de Santa Fe** (*Hebraeomastix*), Moïse Botarel's commentary on the *Sefer Yetzirah* written for a Christian, and crucially **Pedro de la Caballería**'s *Zelus Christi* (c. 1450) — the first Christian text to name the **Zohar** (*Cefer Azohar*, attributed to Simeon bar Yohai), quoting its Aramaic reading of Isaiah 6 as Father (Abba), Son (Bera), and Holy Spirit (Ruha de-Cudsa), and citing Gikatilla's *Sha'are Tsedek* and *Sha'are Ora* (Gates of Justice, Gates of Light) and Nahmanides on the divine names/vertues. He adds **Alfonso de la Torre**'s *Visión deleitable* (printed 1485), whose arithmetic chapter introduces Kabbalah, **Gematria**, and the *Mecubalim*; **Jacobus Pérez de Valencia**'s *Commentaria in Psalmos* (1484, with the *Semiforas*/four-letter name and the Solomon-Temple legend from Kimhi); a Dominican *Censura* of the Talmud; and **Petrus Nigri** (Peter Schwarz), whose *Stern Meshiach* (1475) — the first incunable with Hebrew type — derives the Trinity from the letters of *Bereshit* (Beth=Ben/Son, Resh=Ruach/Spirit, Aleph=Ab/Father). **Pietro Bruto**'s *Victoria* (1489) exploits the closed *Mem* of Isaiah 9. The chapter then climaxes with **Pico**'s famous **Heptaplus** exegesis (1489) of the first word of Genesis, given in **Nicolas Le Fèvre de la Boderie**'s French translation. By permuting the letters of *Bereshit*, Pico extracts twelve words — Ab (Father), Bebar (by/in the Son), Reshit (beginning), Shabbat (rest), Bara (created), Rosh (head), Esh (fire), Shet (foundation), Rab (great), Ish (man), Berith (covenant), Tov (good) — yielding the sentence "the Father by the Son, beginning and end, created the head, the fire and the foundation of the great man by good covenant." This is set within the symbolism of the **four worlds** (intelligences, heavens, elements, microcosm). Secret then gives **Lorenzo da Brindisi**'s expansion of the same device into thirty-three words (for Christ's thirty-three years), and closes echoing Jerome on the hidden treasure of divine wisdom in the Scriptures.
