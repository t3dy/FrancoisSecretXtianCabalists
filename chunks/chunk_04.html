Chunk 4

Pages 13-15

Secret details the diffusion of Ramon Martí's **Pugio Fidei** (first printed 1651 by Joseph de Voisin, used by Pascal, but long circulated in manuscript): Porchetus de Salvaticis's *Victoria adversus Judaeos* (1303), Pierre **Galatino** (accused of plagiarizing the Dominican), Alfonso de **Zamora**'s *Sefer Hokmat Elohim*, and João de Barros. The Pugio drew not on Kabbalah proper but on Talmudic and Midrashic material that overlapped with it; its prologue frames the project as turning the enemy's own weapon — the *Torah she-be'al-peh* (oral law) — against him. He catalogues the recurrent themes. The **"prophecy of Elijah"**: the world lasts 6,000 years — two thousand *Tohu* (void, no law), two thousand under the Law, two thousand for the Messiah — propagated by the *Chronicles of Carion* and used by **Luther** in his *Supputatio annorum mundi* (1541), by Postel, and by Campanella. The **Sem ha-meforash** legend (Jesus stealing the divine name from the Temple to work miracles), reported by Prophiat Duran, who claimed Jesus and the apostles were (erring) kabbalists who misread the Sefirot — *Tiferet* and *Malkhut* — into the Son, Logos, and Holy Spirit, and misunderstood the doctrine of the three lights and the mystery of the "garment." The dialectic of **rigor and mercy** (YHWH = mercy, Elohim = judgment, from *Bereshit Rabba*), and the **12- and 42-letter names** read Trinitarily (Ab/Ben/Ruach ha-qodesh). Secret follows the chain forward: Arnald of Villanova (Martí's disciple), Nicholas of Cusa, and the convergence of Christian curiosity on Kabbalah proper. **Abraham Abulafia** described disciples drawn to Christian heresy through misreading **Temurah** — one of the three letter-combination methods (with **Gematria** and **Notarikon**) encoded in the title *Ginnat Egoz*. **Abner of Burgos** (baptized Alfonso of Valladolid, c. 1320) gives the first attested use of "cabala," in his *Mostrador de Justicia*; **Alfonso de Spina**'s *Fortalitium fidei* (c. 1458) lists the *Mecubalim* (ten sefirot, Metatron, transmigration). The chunk closes on the theme of **metempsychosis/metensomatosis**, which struck Renaissance minds: Marsilio **Ficin** (adapting Paul de Burgos's *Scrutinium fidei*), Ambrogio Fiandino, Elia Levita, and Pierre Viret — the last targeting Postel, who preached his "palingenesis" in 1553.
