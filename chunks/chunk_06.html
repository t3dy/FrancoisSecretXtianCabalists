Chunk 6

Pages 19-22

This chunk consists largely of the **endnotes to Chapter II** — a thick bibliographic apparatus citing Secret's own studies (in *Sefarad*, *B.H.R.*, *Rinascimento*, *Convivium*), Scholem's *Zur Geschichte*, the *Pugio fidei*, Maimonides' *Guide*, the *Fortalitium fidei*, and editions of Pico's *Heptaplus*. The plates reproduced here (the supposed portrait of Mithridates from a Vatican manuscript, pages of his autograph Passion sermon, and a Talmud manuscript translated for Egidio da Viterbo) are described in their captions; the OCR also includes heavily garbled transcriptions of the Latin/Hebrew manuscript pages. **Chapter III, "Pic de la Mirandole et le milieu italien de la kabbale chrétienne,"** then opens. Secret notes that the title of Pico's *Oratio de hominis dignitate* echoes Giannozzo Manetti, a serious Hebraist, but holds that neither Manetti, Bruto, Ficin (who knew a *Bahir* text), nor Ambrogio Traversari actually prepared the flowering of Christian Kabbalah in Italy. That role fell to **two converts**: **Flavius Mithridates** (one of Pico's masters) and **Paul de Heredia**, whom Galatino claimed also taught Pico. Secret reconstructs Mithridates from the *Chronicle of Volterra*: this "Guillaume de Sicile," of the household of the cardinal of Molfetta, learned in Hebrew, Greek, Arabic, and Latin, preached a celebrated two-hour Passion sermon before **Sixtus IV** and the cardinals (including the future Innocent VIII) around 1481, proving Christian mysteries from Hebrew and Arabic sources. He is identified as the convert **Juda Samuel ben Nissim Abul Farag of Girgenti**, who took the name **Guglielmo Raimondo Moncada**. After a mysterious crime (probably a homicide) in 1483, he fled Italy; by 1485 he was in Germany, praised by Rodolph Agricola, teaching Conrad Summenhardt and **Reuchlin**. Summenhardt reports that Mithridates had translated kabbalistic books — the seventy books Pico invoked via Esdras — for Sixtus IV. Secret notes the Passion sermon never uses the word "kabbale" and seems to draw its "ancient Talmudists" from the *Pugio fidei*. Finally, in **1486** Moncada, now **Flavius Mithridates**, met **Pico della Mirandola** and translated kabbalistic manuscripts for him.
