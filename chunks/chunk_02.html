Chunk 2

Pages 5-9

The chunk finishes the **Avant-propos**, where Secret describes his historical-thematic plan (following Valéry Larbaud's filing method): a chapter on Pico's claimed "ancestors," then the beginnings in Spain, the Italian milieu (Flavius Mithridates, Paul de Heredia), Reuchlin in Germany, the Italian golden age (the Ricci, Giustiniani, Galatino, Teseo Ambrogio, Francesco Giorgio Veneto, Egidio da Viterbo), then Germany, France (Postel and his school: the Le Fèvre de la Boderie brothers, Vigenère), Spain, and England, closing with religious reactions, occult philosophy, and the 17th-century afterlife. The **Table des matières** confirms this thirteen-chapter structure. The **Table des planches** lists portraits (Mithridates, Galatino, Postel, Vigenère, the Le Fèvre de la Boderie) and plates such as the Syriac New Testament title page and the "séphirotique vision of St. John." A transcription table then gives the Hebrew alphabet with Renaissance variants and **numerical values** (gematria), with examples drawn from kabbalistic vocabulary: *Bina* (intelligence), *Hesed* (mercy), *Yesod* (foundation), *Keter* (crown), *Sefira*, *Shekinah*. **Chapter I, "Les ancêtres de la kabbale chrétienne,"** opens with Giovanni **Pico della Mirandola** (1463-1494), the "Phénix de son âge," his prodigious memory, his 900 theses *de omni scibili*, and the legend around him (Postel saw himself mirrored in Pico). Secret stresses Pico's declaration in the *Apologia* that he was the first to mention Kabbalah explicitly, corroborated by **Reuchlin** (*De arte cabalistica*, 1517), Egidio da Viterbe, and Pierre **Galatino** (*De arcanis catholicae veritatis*, 1518). Reuchlin coined Latin distinctions: *Cabalici*, *Cabalei*, *Cabalistae*. Secret then quotes Pico's core argument: thesis 9 of the magical conclusions held that nothing proves Christ's divinity more than magic and Kabbalah. Pico defended this by claiming a double Law — the written Pentateuch and a secret oral explanation God gave Moses on Sinai, entrusted only to seventy sages and transmitted by "reception" (the meaning of *kabbalah*). He marshals Christian witnesses — **Esdras** (IV Esdras), **Origen** on Romans (the "oracles of God" / *Torah she-be'al-peh*), **Hilary** on Psalm II (the seventy elders/Sanhedrin), and the Gospel — to legitimate this oral tradition.
