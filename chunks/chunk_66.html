Chunk 66

Pages 205-205

This brief final chunk completes the *Index des noms propres*, providing the last alphabetical entries from S through Z (Stamier, Stancari, Stapleton through Zwinger). The index terminates with figures including Vigenère (a major voice in cabalistic hermeneutics), Vulliaud (20th-century specialist on Kabbalah), Widmanstetter (Renaissance manuscript collector), and Zwinger (Renaissance polymath). The final lines contain the publication colophon: "IMPRIMERIE NOUVELLE, ORLÉANS" with print run information, legal deposit number 4384 for 4th trimester 1963, and the statement "Imprimé en France" (Printed in France). This places the original publication of Secret's *Les kabbalistes chrétiens de la Renaissance* in November-December 1963 by Éditions Dunod (implied from earlier matter), in Orleans typography. The index as a whole (spanning pp. 200-205 across chunks 64-66) documents the dense network of Renaissance and early modern scholars, clergy, mystics, Hebrew linguists, printers, and cabalists whom Secret wove into his narrative of Christian engagement with Kabbalah. It serves as both a working reference and a testament to the scope of his research across manuscript collections, printed editions, and secondary scholarship spanning centuries and continents.
