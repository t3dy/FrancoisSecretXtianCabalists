**The Candelabrum and Tabernacle Symbolism** This section presents detailed samples of Postel's kabbalistic exegesis, focusing on the Candelabrum (Candélabre), a work published in 1547 at Venice that interprets the Temple's seven-branched candlestick as a figure of Christ and the universal cosmic order. In Postel's reading, the chandelier represents the total body of Christ, with its branches corresponding to the seven planets, seven church ages, and the ten divine intelligences distributed across the celestial spheres. The work exemplifies Postel's method: he harmonizes Neoplatonic cosmology with Jewish mysticism, Scripture with astronomical theory, and individual spiritual illumination with collective historical destiny. **Theological Integration of the Ten Sefirot** Postel explicitly develops the kabbalistic doctrine of the ten divine names (Sephiroth) distributed as right (mercy) and left (severity), reiterating the theme of Teshuvah (repentance/return) as the mechanism by which humanity is restored to its original relation to Christ across three ages of law. The fourth and eternal age approaches when the law shall be written in human hearts, a vision explicitly tied to French monarchy and the anticipated Millennium. **The Gallic Messianism and Gomerie** A distinctive feature of Postel's system is his elaboration of Gallic/French identity within a sacred historical schema. He argues that the French kingdom derives from Gomer (son of Japheth), making Gaul—or "Galuyah" (disclosed, elevated to heaven)—the instrument of universal revelation. He coins the neologism "gomerie" (consummation, from Gomer) to describe the final consummation Gomer is charged to achieve. France becomes not merely a political entity but an eschatological agent, and Postel styles himself "sieur de la Gomerie," claiming his prophetic mission is to gomerize—to consummate all things through the revelation of true reason and the Gospel. **Mother Jeanne and the Feminine Principle** Postel's theology incorporates the feminine principle through the figure of Mother Jeanne and the mystical significance of the letter He, the final letter of the divine tetragrammaton YHWH. He argues that Scripture deliberately writes this He in diminished form wherever it appears (over 5,500 times), signifying the union of masculine and feminine nature in Christ and the restoration of humanity through maternalistic wisdom. Mother Jeanne represents this divine feminine intelligence restoring the world. **Elias Redivivus and Transmigration Doctrine** Postel defends his reputation against charges of preaching transmigration by reframing his doctrine: he does not claim migration of individual souls but rather the circulation of the single divine substance (the Spirit of Elijah) through successive human forms across sacred history. Elijah, the greatest of the biblical prophets, manifests in John the Baptist, in Christian saints like Catherine of Siena and Joan of Arc, and finally in Postel himself as Elias redivivus—Elijah restored—whose mission is to reconcile all things and reveal the hidden names of God encoded in the Zohar. **Symbolic Polyvalence and Emithology** Postel invents the term "emithologie" (from Emeth, truth in Hebrew) to describe his hermeneutical method, which reads texts—biblical, kabbalistic, historical, prophetic—as dense multivalent symbols whose true sense is accessible only to the illumined. Everything contains everything; all is in all. This principle of total interpenetration allows Postel to find in the smallest textual detail (a variant spelling, a numerical equivalence, a phonetic similarity) profound cosmic significance. The method is defended against critics as legitimate esoteric wisdom preserved from Moses through the rabbis and now revealed to the Christian world through the Zohar's translation. This chunk presents Postel's mature synthesis: a comprehensive metaphysical and historical vision in which Jewish mysticism, French monarchy, Christian eschatology, feminine spirituality, and personal prophetic consciousness fuse into a single interpretive system aimed at universal reconciliation.
