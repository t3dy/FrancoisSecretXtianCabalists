Pages 79-81

Giorgio of Venice develops the concept that spiritual ascent proceeds through the ten celestial spheres (Sephiroth) above which three supernal luminaries reign—interpreted as the Trinity and as the three supreme Patriarchs (Abraham, Isaac, Jacob). The earthly Tabernacle, shown to Moses, serves as a model for understanding both celestial realms and human nature as the microcosm (*Olam Katon*, the "small world" or "world of separation"). This teaching grounds itself in extended quotations from the Zohar and Bahya ben Asher's commentary on Exodus, which reveals that above the ten Sephiroth exist three luminaries that precede all creation, constituting "name and substance at the root of roots." Giorgio establishes that the universe was created through two divine attributes—judgment and mercy—paralleled by the two architect-artisans of the Tabernacle (Bezalel and Aholiab). All elements of the cosmic and natural orders thus reflect each other in a unified system, an ancient philosophical principle (attributed to Anaxagoras and Hippocrates) that Hebrew mystics perfected. The sacred language of Hebrew possesses intrinsic mystical power: its twenty-two letters, combined with the ten Sephiroth, produce thirty-two paths of Wisdom through which God created the world. Three mother letters (Aleph, Mem, Shin) govern elements; seven double letters (Begadkephrath) govern planets; twelve simple letters govern zodiacal signs. Giorgio demonstrates mastery of gematria (numerical values): the addition of *He* to Abram's name (243) produces Abraham (248), equaling the number of human bones and of affirmative commandments in the Torah. He identifies Metatron, the celestial Prince of Faces, through numerical calculation (999, later 290), then shows that these same values attach to Miriam/Mary (290 in Hebrew), arguing that the name Metatron represents an "office" rather than a person, thus applicable to the Virgin as intermediary before God's throne. The section culminates in Giorgio's complex numerology of the divine name Jesus (YSU), wherein the Tetragrammaton (YHWH) is fulfilled and the number six (the first perfect number, denoting creation) ensures that all cosmic creation participates in the Incarnate Word.
