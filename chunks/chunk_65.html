Chunk 65

Pages 201-204

This chunk presents the first major section of an extensive *Index des noms propres* (Index of Proper Names), covering entries alphabetically from A through P (Abarbanel through Puteanus, pp. 201-203). The index lists approximately 400+ names with page references, serving as a comprehensive guide to all personages mentioned throughout Secret's monograph. The names span multiple categories: Jewish medieval and early modern figures (*Bahya ben Asher, Abulafia, Abarbanel family, Abraham de Balmes*); Christian humanist and kabbalist scholars (*Pic de la Mirandole, Reuchlin, Gilles de Viterbe, Giorgio Venerio, Postel, Gaffarel*); Christian theologians and clergy (*Jerome, Augustine, Bérulle, Coton, Jesuit and Dominican figures*); European intellectuals of the 16th-17th centuries (*Erasmus, Luther, Calvin, Valla, Bodin, Scaliger, Kepler, Campanella, Paracelsus*); printers and textual scholars (*Bomberg, Plantin, Giustiniani*); and numerous lesser-known grammarians, Hebrew linguists, and cabalists scattered across the universities and religious communities of Europe. The index reveals Secret's sources encompass a wide geographic and confessional range: Spanish conversos, Italian humanists, Northern European Reformers, French royal officials and cardinals, German cabalists, Polish and Bohemian scholars. The frequency of page references for certain figures (like Pic, Reuchlin, Postel, Gaffarel, and Giorgio Venerio) identifies them as major subjects of the book's narrative. The index thus functions both as a reference tool and as an implicit map of the Christian Kabbalah movement's scope and personnel across nearly two centuries and multiple European regions.
