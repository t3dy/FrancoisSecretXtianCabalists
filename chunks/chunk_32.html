Chunk 32

Pages 101-103

This chunk traces **Postel's** literary productivity and doctrine. His Latin **Zohar translation**, long thought lost, was partly recovered: an érudit found a copy, and Secret himself located the volumes (some 350 in-folio pages) at the **Göttingen** library; other Postel manuscripts entered the **Sloane** collection via Basel and the printer **Oporin**. Postel retranslated the Zohar in 1563, entrusting the deposit to his legatee **Le Fèvre de la Boderie**. In his Venetian years he was the "véloce scribe" or **tachygraphe**, translating the **Bahir**, part of **Recanati**'s commentary, writing on Genesis and Ruth, and producing works like the *Absconditorum clavis* and *De nativitate mediatoris ultima*. He even served briefly as censor of Hebrew books for the nuncio Della Casa. After acquiring Arabic and Syriac manuscripts (via **Gabriel d'Aramon**'s embassy in Jerusalem) for a projected **polyglot Bible**, he returned to find Mère Jeanne dead. In 1552 Postel underwent his **"Immutation"**: receiving the "survêtement" promised by Mère Jeanne, he became **Caïn restitué, son of the New Eve**, claiming bodily immortality (food dissipating into air). This crisis produced a flood of works (*La Restitution de toutes choses*, etc.), all proclaiming his mission as **Élie who restores all things**. Persecution drove him through Besançon, Basel, Vienna (where he aided the Syriac New Testament edition), then Venice, where the **Inquisition** tried him, declared him *amens* (insane), and imprisoned him in Rome until Paul IV's death; finally he was relegated to **Saint-Martin-des-Champs** until 1581. Secret then summarizes Postel's **system**: a **millenarianism** in which world and man, created by the **Mediator** between immobile God and the mobile world, pass through four ages—law of nature, written Law, law of grace, and the **Concorde** (man restored to pre-lapsarian reason). Postel claimed two kabbalistic truths: the **spirit of the Messiah** and **Gilgul** (recirculation of souls), condensed in "Omnia in omnibus." This Messiah-spirit was one with Adam, Hénoch, Élie, Moïse, and—in feminine aspect—the **Venetian Virgin**. The October motif (Yom Kippur/Assereth, the second Pâque, the deluge, Janus/Noah) recurs across the *De Etruriæ*. Crucially, Postel held the **Zohar** to be the work of **Symeon the Just**, the Simeon of Luke 2 who received the infant Jesus; glossing the **Lek Leka** section, he read Rabbi Abba's words as veiling Christ's revelation at **Lud** (= *Veled*, embryo/birth), later redacted by Symeon ben Iochai under Antoninus Pius.
